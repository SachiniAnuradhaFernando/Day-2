    Step 1: Cleanse

    Start by washing your face with a gentle cleanser to remove dirt, oil, and impurities. Choose a cleanser suitable for your skin type (dry, oily, combination, or sensitive).

    • Use lukewarm water, not hot or cold.
    • Massage the cleanser into your skin in circular motions.
    • Rinse thoroughly and pat your face dry with a clean towel.


    Step 2: Tone

    Toning helps to balance your skin's pH and prepare it for better absorption of products. Use a toner with hydrating or calming properties.

    • Apply toner using a cotton pad or your hands.
    • Gently pat the toner onto your face.
    • Allow the toner to absorb fully before moving to the next step.


    Step 3: Moisturize

    Moisturizing is essential to keep your skin hydrated and soft. Choose a moisturizer suitable for your skin type.

    • Take a small amount of moisturizer and apply it to your face and neck.
    • Gently massage in upward motions until fully absorbed.


    Step 4: Sunscreen

    Always finish your morning routine with sunscreen to protect your skin from harmful UV rays. Even on cloudy days, sunscreen is important to prevent premature aging and skin damage.

    • Choose a sunscreen with at least SPF 30.
    • Apply it generously to your face and neck.
    • Reapply every two hours if you're outdoors for extended periods.


    Common Skincare Mistakes

    Even with a good routine, these common mistakes can hinder your skin's progress:

    • Over-exfoliating: Too much exfoliation can damage your skin barrier. Stick to 1-2 times per week.
    • Skipping sunscreen: Sunscreen is essential daily, no matter the weather.
    • Using too many products: Keep your routine simple, especially as a beginner.


    Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

    1. How often should I wash my face?

    Twice a day—once in the morning and once at night—is enough to keep your skin clean and healthy.

    2. Can I skip toner?

    Toner can be skipped if you’re short on time, but it can enhance your routine by balancing the skin’s pH and preparing it for moisturizer.

    3. How much sunscreen should I apply?

    Use a generous amount, about the size of a nickel for your face. Make sure to reapply every two hours when outdoors.
